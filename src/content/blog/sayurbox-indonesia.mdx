# Sayurbox

Indonesia's farm-to-table online grocery platform leveraged Google Ads to scale operations to over 3,000 orders per day.

## The Challenge

Sayurbox launched with a mission to connect Indonesian consumers directly with local farmers, offering fresh produce delivered to doorsteps. While the concept resonated with health-conscious consumers in Jakarta and other major cities, scaling an online grocery business in Indonesia presented unique obstacles. The online grocery penetration rate in Indonesia was low, and most consumers still preferred shopping at traditional wet markets or supermarkets.

Building consumer trust for fresh produce delivery was particularly difficult — shoppers worried about freshness, delivery timing, and whether the quality would match what they could hand-pick at a physical market. Sayurbox needed to not only acquire new customers but also educate them on the reliability and convenience of online grocery shopping.

## The Solution

Sayurbox deployed a multi-channel Google Ads strategy encompassing Search, Display, and YouTube to address different stages of the customer journey — from awareness to acquisition to retention.

<div class="callout callout-info">

**Technical Approach:** Search campaigns targeted high-intent queries like "belanja sayur online" (buy vegetables online) and "buah segar antar" (fresh fruit delivery). Display campaigns with vibrant product imagery reached audiences browsing food, recipe, and health-related content. YouTube pre-roll ads showcased the farm-to-table journey, building trust by showing consumers exactly where their food came from.

</div>

The team implemented value-based conversion tracking, weighting first-time orders more heavily than repeat purchases to help the algorithm focus on new customer acquisition. They also used Customer Match to suppress existing customers from acquisition campaigns and create lookalike audiences for prospecting.

Seasonal campaigns around Ramadan, Eid, and other Indonesian holidays were critical drivers of volume, with tailored ad copy and landing pages featuring holiday-specific bundles and promotions. Sayurbox scaled budgets aggressively during these peak periods, knowing that first-time holiday customers often converted into regular buyers.

Reaching 3,000+ daily orders marked a milestone for Sayurbox, proving that online grocery could scale in Indonesia. Google Ads served as the primary engine for customer acquisition during the company's growth phase, enabling Sayurbox to expand beyond Jakarta into cities like Surabaya and Bandung. The multi-channel approach was essential — Search captured existing demand, while Display and YouTube created new demand by educating consumers about the benefits of farm-to-table delivery.

## Key Takeaway

<div class="callout callout-tip">

**For online grocery and fresh delivery startups:** In markets where your category has low penetration, you need both demand capture (Search) and demand creation (YouTube, Display). Use video to build trust around product quality and freshness. Time your biggest budget pushes around cultural moments and holidays when consumers are most open to trying new shopping methods.
