# Traveloka

Southeast Asia's largest travel platform used Dynamic Search Ads and Smart Bidding powered by first-party data to improve ROAS by 11% and booking value by 14%.

## The Challenge

Traveloka is Southeast Asia's leading travel and lifestyle platform, offering flights, hotels, activities, and financial services across multiple countries. With millions of product listings that change dynamically — hotel prices fluctuate, flight availability shifts constantly, and new activities are added daily — maintaining comprehensive keyword coverage through traditional Search campaigns was an enormous operational burden.

The scale of Traveloka's inventory meant that manual keyword management would always lag behind the real-time nature of travel supply and demand. The company also needed to differentiate between high-value bookings (premium hotels, international flights) and lower-margin transactions to allocate ad spend efficiently. Without value-based optimization, ad dollars were spread evenly regardless of revenue potential.

## The Solution

Traveloka adopted a two-pronged approach: Dynamic Search Ads (DSA) for coverage and Smart Bidding with first-party data for optimization. This combination addressed both the breadth and depth challenges simultaneously.

<div class="callout callout-info">

**Technical Approach:** DSA campaigns automatically generated ads by crawling Traveloka's website, matching user queries to relevant product pages — flights, hotels, activities — without requiring explicit keyword lists. This ensured coverage for long-tail queries like "hotel near Tanah Lot Bali under 500k IDR" that would be impossible to anticipate manually. Smart Bidding (Maximize Conversion Value with target ROAS) layered on top, using Traveloka's first-party booking data to bid more aggressively for high-value transactions.

</div>

The integration of first-party data was critical. Traveloka fed actual booking revenue, customer lifetime value signals, and product margin data into their conversion tracking. This allowed Smart Bidding to understand that a premium hotel booking in Bali was worth significantly more than a budget hostel reservation — and bid accordingly.

The team also used audience layering within DSA campaigns, adjusting bid multipliers for returning customers, loyalty program members, and users who had previously booked premium products. This first-party data enrichment gave the bidding algorithm additional context to make better decisions.

## Key Takeaway

<div class="callout callout-tip">

**For large inventory platforms:** Dynamic Search Ads solve the coverage problem — let Google match your massive product catalog to user queries automatically. The combination of automation (DSA) and intelligence (first-party data + Smart Bidding) is the blueprint for efficient performance marketing at scale.
