# AirAsia

Southeast Asia's largest low-cost carrier used Google Dynamic Search Ads to capture long-tail travel demand, boosting bookings by 21% and revenue by 11%.

## The Challenge

AirAsia operates hundreds of routes across Asia-Pacific, with constantly changing fares, schedules, and promotions. The airline's search advertising challenge was fundamentally one of scale and dynamism — maintaining keyword coverage for every route, destination, and fare combination was operationally unsustainable. New routes launched regularly, seasonal promotions changed weekly, and fare prices shifted multiple times per day.

Traditional keyword-based campaigns covered core routes and branded terms well but missed the vast long tail of travel queries — specific date combinations, multi-city itineraries, and niche destination searches. These long-tail queries often carried higher conversion intent because travelers searching for specific routes and dates were closer to booking than those browsing generally.

## The Solution

AirAsia implemented Dynamic Search Ads (DSA) to complement their existing keyword campaigns. DSA automatically matched user search queries to the most relevant pages on AirAsia's website, generating ad headlines dynamically based on page content.

<div class="callout callout-info">

**Technical Approach:** AirAsia configured DSA campaigns using page feeds — curated lists of URLs organized by route category, destination region, and promotion type. This gave the team control over which pages could trigger DSA ads while still benefiting from Google's automated query matching. Negative keywords were used to prevent overlap with existing keyword campaigns, ensuring DSA served as a true incremental channel focused on capturing unaddressed demand.

</div>

The airline set up dynamic ad targets based on their website's URL structure, allowing campaigns to automatically adapt as new routes and promotions were published. When AirAsia launched a new route or promotional fare, DSA picked it up automatically — eliminating the delay between marketing and advertising.

Smart Bidding was layered on top of the DSA campaigns, using Maximize Conversions with a target CPA to ensure the incremental traffic was acquired efficiently. The team monitored search term reports closely during the initial ramp-up phase to identify new keyword opportunities and refine negative keyword lists.

The 21% increase in bookings and 11% revenue lift demonstrated the significant incremental value hiding in long-tail search queries that keyword campaigns alone could not capture. For an airline operating at AirAsia's scale, these percentage improvements translated to hundreds of thousands of additional bookings annually. DSA also reduced the operational burden on the marketing team, freeing them from the constant cycle of keyword updates and campaign restructuring.

## Key Takeaway

<div class="callout callout-tip">

**For airlines and travel companies with large, dynamic inventories:** Dynamic Search Ads are essential for capturing the long tail of route-specific queries. Use page feeds for control, negative keywords to prevent cannibalization with existing campaigns, and Smart Bidding for efficiency. The key insight is that DSA should complement — not replace — your keyword campaigns, focusing on the incremental demand your manual campaigns miss.
